# How to Play VEXED!

## The Basics

Letters sit on a grid. Tap a tile to select it, then swipe in any direction — the tile slides until it hits a wall or another tile.

When tiles line up to spell a valid word (reading left→right or top→bottom), a **gold outline** appears around that word. Tap any tile inside the outline to collect it. The tiles vanish and your score goes up.

That's the whole game. The twist is the vowels.

---

## The Vowel Rule

If **3 or more of the same vowel touch** — in any shape, horizontally or vertically — they all vanish instantly. No warning. No score.

Keeping your vowels spread out is the hidden skill of VEXED!

---

## Scoring

| Word length | Base points |
|---|---|
| 3 letters | 30 |
| 4 letters | 40 |
| 5 letters | 70 (bonus kicks in) |
| 6 letters | 80 |
| 7 letters | 90 |
| +1 letter | +10 pts |

Longer words are worth disproportionately more — a 5-letter word scores more than one 3-letter and one 2-letter word combined.

### Combo Multiplier

Score words back-to-back without losing vowels and your multiplier climbs:

| Consecutive words | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| 1 | ×1.0 |
| 2 | ×1.5 |
| 3 | ×2.0 |
| 4+ | ×3.0 |

Lose vowels or run out of moves and the combo resets to zero.

---

## Tile Forge

Every word you collect forges new tiles into empty spaces on the board. Longer words forge more tiles — this is how you keep the board alive.

The number of tiles forged depends on **both the word length and your difficulty**:

**Forge tiles = word length − threshold (no cap, keeps increasing)**

| Word length | Easy (−1) | Medium (−2) | Hard (−3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 letters | +2 tiles | +1 tile | — |
| 4 letters | +3 tiles | +2 tiles | +1 tile |
| 5 letters | +4 tiles | +3 tiles | +2 tiles |
| 6 letters | +5 tiles | +4 tiles | +3 tiles |
| 7 letters | +6 tiles | +5 tiles | +4 tiles |

On **Easy**, even short words keep feeding the board. On **Hard**, only 4-letter words and above forge tiles — and you need longer words to forge more than a trickle. The board won't save you; your vocabulary has to.

Forged tiles are randomly generated (weighted toward vowels if the board is running low on them).

---

## Word Strip

The strip at the bottom shows every word you can score **right now** without moving a single tile. Chips are sorted longest-first — longest words are worth the most.

- **Tap a chip** to highlight those tiles on the board and see the word's definition.
- **Tap a highlighted tile** on the board to collect that word.

Red chips are uncollected. After a word is scored, it moves to the history panel at the end.

---

## Danger Warning

When a cluster of identical vowels is growing close to 3, the board pulses with a coloured warning. Each vowel has its own colour:

- **Red** — A cluster
- **Blue** — E cluster
- **Green** — I cluster
- **Orange** — O cluster
- **Purple** — U cluster

If you see a pulse, slide one of those vowels away before the group reaches 3.

---

## Game Over

The game ends when **no slide can form a word**, even looking two moves ahead. You'll see a grade based on how much of the board's total potential score you captured:

| Grade | Score captured |
|---|---|
| S | 90%+ |
| A | 75–89% |
| B | 60–74% |
| C | 45–59% |
| D | 30–44% |
| F | Below 30% |

---

## Difficulty Levels

### Easy — 5×5 grid
- 12,500 everyday English words
- Minimum word length: 3 letters
- Forge threshold: −1 (generous — even 3-letter words give +2 tiles)
- Best for learning the mechanics

### Medium — 7×7 grid
- 33,000 words (includes less common vocabulary)
- Minimum word length: 3 letters
- Forge threshold: −2 (balanced — 3-letter words give +1 tile)
- The intended experience

### Hard — 10×10 grid
- 132,000 words (full English dictionary including rare and archaic words)
- Minimum word length: **4 letters** (3-letter words don't count)
- Forge threshold: −3 (punishing — you need 4+ letters to forge anything)
- Chain long words or the board slowly starves

---

## Tips

- **Think before you slide.** Tiles move fast; vowels vanish faster.
- **Check the word strip first.** There may already be words to collect before you move anything.
- **Spread vowels deliberately.** Two Es side by side is fine. Three is catastrophe.
- **Longer words pay double.** A 6-letter word scores roughly as much as two 3-letter words *and* forges more tiles.
- **Combos matter most on Hard.** A ×3.0 multiplier on a 7-letter word is your path to a top grade.
